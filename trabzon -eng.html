  • Sumela Monastery

    • The Sümela Monastery, built on a steep cliff in the foothills of Montenegro, dominating the Altındere valley, within the borders of Altındere Village of Maçka District of Trabzon, is known as the "Virgin Mary" among the people. The structure, which is about 300 meters above the valley, has continued the tradition of establishing monasteries outside the city, in forests, caves and waterfront. It is said that the monastery, founded in the name of the Virgin Mary, took its name "Sumela" from the word "molasses" meaning "black".

  • Hamsikoy

    • Don't be fooled by the name Hamsiköy, which is located on the foothills of the Zigana Mountains at an altitude of 1300 meters, this place is famous for its rice pudding, not its anchovies. Its name comes from "hamse", which means 5 in Arabic. In other words, the original name of the village is Hamsköy or Hamseköy. It is a collective name given to five villages in this region. These five villages: Hamsiköy, Ciharlı, Çıralı, Dikkaya and Güzelyayla.

  • Akçaabat Patties

    • Akçaabat patties are at the beginning of the delicacies that must be consumed when you arrive in Trabzon. Among the many meatballs found in our country, its reputation is one of the flavors that transcends the borders of the country. The most important difference of this meatball from the other is that there is no nerve and extra fat in the meat.

  • Trabzon Pita

    • In our country, many different varieties of pita bread made in Trabzon that type of language is epic. All materials of pita are produced naturally. Dough of pita bread prepared using local cheese, meat, flour is opened by hand.

  • Trabzon Tea

    • It is almost impossible to come to Trabzon and return without drinking tea. Tea is the first treat that will be offered to you when you visit a restaurant or anywhere along the road. Vast tea plantations were first created in the 1930s. It is recommended that you consume the products of these fields, which are taken three times a year.

  • Stuffed Acorn

    • One of the delicacies that those who say I eat what comes out of the sea should try in Trabzon is the Dolmasi of Acorn. It is a great dish that comes with the addition of internal mortar.
