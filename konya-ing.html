  • Catalhoyuk Neolithic City

    • Catalhoyuk, described as one of the oldest settlements of the Neolithic period, sheds light on human history with its original finds such as first house architecture, first landscape painting, cult of the mother goddess and works of faithst house architecture, first landscape painting, mother goddess cult and works of faith. Catalhoyuk, located in Çumra District of Konya province, was discovered in 1958. It is believed that Catalhoyuk's Neolithic settlement style and urban plan reflect the ideals of equality. As of 2012, Catalhoyuk has been included in the UNESCO World Heritage list.

  • Kilistra Antic City

    • Kilistra ancient city', located on the historical 'King Road' in Konya and drawing attention with its fairy chimneys-like structure in Cappadocia, takes its visitors on a historical journey. Kilistra, which has structures such as a cross-planned chapel, hyacinth church, large water cistern, churches, monasteries, watchtowers, shelters, ancient roads, is also an important center of faith tourism.

  • Çebiç

    • Chebic is one of the most local delicacies of Konya. In addition, it can be said that it is very interesting. If you want to experience a very different breakfast in Konya, chebic may be for you. If you are going to eat chebic, you need to be in a guest house for breakfast, because the process of cooking on the grill to get lamb liver is quite long.

  • Meat Bread

    • The first flavor that comes to mind when it is called Konya is meat bread. It is not known why meat bread is so identified with Konya, but it is questioned whether it is Konya style when it is called meat bread, no matter what corner of our country you experience today. In order to taste the most beautiful meat bread experience, it is recommended to eat meat bread in Konya, the homeland of this taste. Although it is usually confused with lahmacun because it has a very similar appearance, the taste of meat bread is really different from lahmacun.

  • Mevlana Pastry

    • Mevlana pastry is one of the breakfast dishes unique to Konya. To experience this breakfast, you can go to the breakfast venues. Konya Mevlana pie has its own unique image. It is a pastry prepared in a triangular shape and has an inner mortar inside. After eating Mevlana pastry, where it is possible to consume it piecemeal, you may have difficulty stopping.

  • Dilber Lip

    • The name of this flavor, whose appearance is as appetizing as the taste, is Dilber Lip dessert. Dilberdudağı, which we can show as the Shah of sorbet desserts, is prepared by placing plenty of walnuts in pieces of dough shaped like tiny sandwiches. This crispy baklava, which is included in the list of regional dishes of Konya, is fried in the oven and then sherbet and takes its place on the tables.

  • Höşmerim Dessert
