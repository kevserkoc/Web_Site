  • Aspendos Ancient City

    • Aspendos Ancient City, 2 BC. it was built by the Romans in the century and is one of the most beautifully preserved theatres in history. This theater was built in the form of an open-air theater and the construction of the theater began in approximately 138 years. It is among the rare structures that can be preserved to this day. The architect of the theater is Zenon, the son of Theodoros of Aspendos. Today, this theater hosts many various concerts.

  • Ancient City Of Perge

    • Perge ancient city is located in Aksu district, 17 kilometers east of Antalya city center. The city, which is believed to have existed in the Hittite period and is known as "Parha", was one of the most regular cities in Anatolia during the Roman period. The sculptures unearthed during the excavations in the ancient city, which is famous for its architecture and marble sculpture, have made the Antalya Museum one of the most important sculpture museums.

  • Cretan Kebab With Artichokes

    • Cretan kebab with artichokes is a taste that is seen as a fruit of Greek culture, but is also loved and consumed in Antalya and is now localized. It is a very interesting taste made with chopped meat, onions and artichokes. Currently, people can also add meatballs to it when making Cretan kebab with artichokes. But remember that the flavor is essentially made with diced meats.

  • Çiğirdik

    • Çiğirdik is one of the snacks usually consumed next to tea in Antalya. As a snack, we can say that it is an interesting flavor that is constantly made. The taste of square snacks, prepared only with sesame and grape molasses, also makes itself eaten as it eats. Sesame desserts are sold by peddlers on the streets in some cities, such as Ankara. If you like croquettes with honey and molasses, çiğirdik, a local croquant, can be a great snack option for you. It should be said that it is also dense in terms of calories.

  • Pumpkin Dessert

    • Pumpkin dessert, served on New Year and holidays, is one of the famous desserts of Antalya. Especially on crowded tables, it is served with cream and ice cream.
