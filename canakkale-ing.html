  • Canakkale Martyrdom and Martyrs Monument

    • During the first visit to Çanakkale, of course, the martyrdom of Çanakkale comes. Dardanelles martyrdom and Martyrs Monument, opened in 1960, was built on behalf of Mehmetçikler who died in the Dardanelles war in 1915. Located in the Gallipoli Peninsula National Park, the monument is one of the most important visiting points of the region.

  • Truva (Troya) Antic City

    • The ancient city of Troy, also known as Troy, is a historical city that was the scene of the Battle of Troy mentioned in The Iliad saga of Homer.

  • Trojan Horse

    • The famous Trojan Horse, mentioned in the mythology of wool and the saga of Homer, appeared after Odysseus ' ingenious plan to end the Trojan War. It is believed that this famous battle took place on the territory of Çanakkale.

  • Kilitbahir Castle

    • Kilitbahir, a village in the Eceabat District of Canakkale, is located in the narrowest part of the Strait in the south of the Gallipoli peninsula. Kilitbahir, as a name, means ”sea lock”. The important feature of the village is that it has Kilitbahir Castle, a large castle in the form of three-leaf clover built by Fatih Sultan Mehmet.

  • Assos (Assos Antic City)

    • Assos, located in Ayvacık district of Çanakkale, is an ancient city located in Behram Village. Assos, in addition to its historical value, is one of the first resorts that come to mind when it is called summer holidays in Turkey.

  • Tumbi

    • Tumbi, one of the most special tastes of Canakkale, starts our list of Canakkale regional dishes. Tumbi, which contains a lot of vegetables and pulses, is a very satisfying and delicious meal. It contains a kind of wheat called göce. That's where the food comes from. Charming with the flavor of Çanakkale, the dish is prepared with Tumbi, göce, eggplant, onion, parsley and tomato.

  • Flap

    • Next up is a delicious snack from Çanakkale cuisine. Although it is similar to spinach pie in appearance, it is a very different dish with both the stage of construction and the taste. Although it can be prepared with pumpkin on request, there is spinach in the main whisk. Whipping, which is a full store of vitamins with spinach in it, is also very satisfying when made from dough.

  • Biga Patties

    • You've probably eaten a lot of meatballs with the famous word in front of your name. But after you eat this meatball, you will realize that the meatballs you eat have no characteristics. Meatballs prepared with careful selection, from meat to other ingredients used in them, are a flavor that someone who once tried can no longer give up. Biga patties, which have been one of the most important tastes of the city for nearly a century, also have an important place in terms of Canakkale gastronomic tourism.

  • Bluefish Rice

    • Although rice is generally known as chicken, meat or chickpea, rice is made in the Çanakkale region along with bluefish, the favorite fish of the Çanakkale cuisine. Don't say Fish and rice. This dish proves how good rice is with fish.

  • Basma Halva

    • After the exquisite dishes of Canakkale, it's time for desserts that make a difference with their palate-cracking flavors. In the first place is a halva, which is very loved by everyone. No matter what semolina or flour is obviously made with, all kinds of halva have an important place in traditional Turkish cuisine. Basma halva, the favorite of Canakkale local desserts, is a great complement to Canakkale tables, especially after fish dishes.

  • Mafis

    • Made in many parts of our country, in some regions, dough fries are also known as pishi in some regions, the recipe is called Mafis in Canakkale. Especially in the morning breakfast without waiting for the dough to ferment Mafis, a recipe that can be made immediately when you crave fried dough.
